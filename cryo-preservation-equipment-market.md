<p><strong>The Cryo Preservation Equipment Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Liquid Phase</li><li>Vapor Phase</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cryo preservation equipment operates primarily in two market types: liquid phase and vapor phase. </p><p>Liquid phase cryopreservation involves storing biological samples directly in liquid nitrogen at extremely low temperatures, ensuring maximum viability. This method is often used for cell cultures, tissues, and embryos. </p><p>Vapor phase, on the other hand, stores samples above the liquid nitrogen surface, allowing for easier sample retrieval and reduced risk of cross-contamination. This method is gaining traction for sensitive biological materials that need careful handling.</p></p>
