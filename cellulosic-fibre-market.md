<p><p>The cellulosic fiber market is divided into two primary types: natural cellulose fibers and man-made cellulose fibers. Natural cellulose fibers, such as cotton, linen, and hemp, are derived from plant sources and are valued for their biodegradability and breathability. In contrast, man-made cellulose fibers, including viscose, lyocell, and acetate, are produced by chemically processing wood pulp or other cellulose-rich materials. These fibers are appreciated for their versatility, softness, and ability to mimic the properties of natural fibers, catering to diverse textile applications.</p></p>
