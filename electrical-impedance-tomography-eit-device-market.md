<p><ul><li>Static EIT</li><li>Dynamic EIT</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Electrical Impedance Tomography (EIT) devices are categorized into two main market types: Static EIT and Dynamic EIT. Static EIT focuses on creating images based on a stable conductivity distribution within the body, often used for applications like breast imaging. In contrast, Dynamic EIT captures real-time changes in electrical impedance, making it suitable for monitoring physiological processes, such as lung ventilation and cardiac activity. Both types leverage electrical impedance for medical diagnostics, but their applications and techniques differ significantly.</p></p>
